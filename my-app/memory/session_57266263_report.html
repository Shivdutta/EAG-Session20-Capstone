Adjustment: Phase-Based Rebalancing Strategy

Rebalancing is the disciplined process of realigning the portfolio to its strategic asset allocation. It is critical for managing risk and is not a one-time event but an ongoing process governed by clear rules.

  • Frequency: A full portfolio review should be conducted annually.
  • Triggers: Rebalancing should be executed under two conditions: (1) Calendar-based: Annually, to ensure alignment. (2) Trigger-based: If either the Equity or Debt allocation deviates by more than 5% from its target (e.g., Equity grows to 65% of the portfolio).
  • Methodology:
    - For Minor Deviations: Use new SIP inflows to rebalance. For example, if equity is overweight, direct a larger portion of the next few SIPs into the underweight debt funds.
    - For Major Deviations & Phased De-risking: This requires actively selling/redeeming units. In Phase 3, you will systematically sell from over-performing equity funds and reinvest the proceeds into the designated debt funds to adhere to the de-risking glide path.
Worked Example: At the end of Year 5, a bull market has caused the equity portion to grow to 66% of the portfolio value. The rebalancing trigger is met. The investor should redeem 6% of the portfolio value from the equity funds (proportionally) and reinvest that amount into the debt funds to bring the allocation back to the strategic 60/40 split.
