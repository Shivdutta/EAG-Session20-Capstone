🎯 Executive Summary

Asian Paints Ltd. is India's largest paint manufacturer and ranks among the top 10 decorative coatings companies globally. Established in 1942, the company has grown into a global leader operating in over 15 countries and serving consumers in more than 60 markets. With a market share of over 50% in the Indian paint market, Asian Paints dominates the industry through its innovative products, strong brand equity, and wide distribution network [1].

The company operates on a B2C and B2B model, catering to individual homeowners, contractors, architects, and industrial clients. Its diversified product portfolio includes decorative paints, industrial coatings, waterproofing solutions, and home improvement and decor products. Asian Paints has a robust supply chain and an extensive distribution network with over 160,000 retail touchpoints across India [2].

Financially, Asian Paints has shown sustained growth, although recent results indicate some challenges. The company's revenue for FY25 was INR 33,905.62 Cr, compared to INR 35,494.73 Cr in FY24. Net profit also saw a decrease from INR 5,424.69 Cr in FY24 to INR 3,569.00 Cr in FY25. Despite these challenges, Asian Paints remains a strong player in the market, driven by its innovation and market leadership [3].

🏢 Company Overview

Asian Paints was founded in 1942 by Champaklal Choksey, Chimanlal Choksi, Suryakant Dani, and Arvind Vakil. The company was conceived in Mumbai with the idea of challenging foreign makeup giants in the Indian market. Initially named Asian Oil and Paint Company Pvt. Ltd., it focused on surface coatings and latex for rubber tires [4].

Over the decades, Asian Paints expanded its operations and product range, becoming a public limited company in 1973. By 1967, it had established itself as the market leader in India's paint industry. Today, it is the second-largest paint manufacturer in Asia and ranks eighth globally, with 27 worldwide manufacturing facilities [5].

Business Model

Asian Paints operates on both B2C and B2B models, catering to individual homeowners, contractors, architects, and industrial clients. The company's business model is vertically integrated, encompassing manufacturing, distribution, and sales of paints, coatings, and home decor products [6].

Key components of the business model include:

  • Decorative Coatings: Core revenue stream from interior and exterior paints.
  • Home Decor and Adjacent Categories: Diversification into waterproofing solutions, adhesives, wall coverings, and modular kitchens.
  • Extensive Distribution Network: Over 1.69 lakh retail touchpoints in India.
  • Innovation and Product Development: Focus on R&D to introduce premium offerings.
  • International Presence: Operations in 15 countries with localized strategies.

Revenue Streams

Asian Paints generates revenue through multiple streams:

  • Decorative Paints: Primary revenue driver, including interior, exterior, and wood/metal finishes.
  • Industrial Coatings: Supplying paints and coatings to automotive, packaging, and infrastructure industries.
  • Waterproofing Solutions: Sealants, roof coatings, and cementitious solutions.
  • Home Improvement and Decor: Modular kitchens, wardrobes, furniture, and renovation services.
  • Adhesives and Sealants: Tile adhesives, wood glues, and bonding solutions.
