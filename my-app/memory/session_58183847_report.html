⚠️ Risk Factors and Uncertainties

While this plan is built on sound financial principles, it is subject to various risks and uncertainties that must be considered. Market Risk is the most significant factor; the assumed 10% annual return is an estimate, not a guarantee. Equity markets are volatile and can experience downturns, potentially leading to a shortfall if poor performance persists. Interest Rate Risk primarily affects the debt portion of the portfolio. A rise in interest rates can lead to a fall in the value of existing bonds and bond funds. Furthermore, Inflation Risk poses a threat if the actual rate of inflation exceeds the projected 5.5%, thereby eroding the real value of the final corpus and requiring a larger investment to maintain the desired purchasing power. There is also Concentration Risk noted in the fund reconciliation, where only a single fund option was identified for the 'Government Securities' category, limiting choice. Lastly, Execution Risk lies with the investor; failing to invest regularly or reacting emotionally to market swings can significantly jeopardize the achievement of the financial goal. A disciplined, long-term perspective is crucial for mitigating these behavioral risks.
