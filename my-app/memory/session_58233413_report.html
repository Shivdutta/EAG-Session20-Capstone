⚖️ Risk Analysis & Mitigation

While this portfolio is diversified and aligned with a 'Moderate' risk profile, it is essential to understand the inherent risks and the strategies in place to mitigate them. This plan is exposed to several key risks, which have been carefully considered in its construction.

  • Market Risk: With an initial 55% allocation to equities, the portfolio is subject to market fluctuations and volatility. A significant market downturn, particularly in the early years, could temporarily impede corpus growth. Mitigation: The 10-year investment horizon provides ample time for recovery from market cycles. The portfolio's core is composed of Large Cap funds, which are typically less volatile than broader equity markets. The glide path actively reduces this risk by lowering equity exposure as retirement nears.
  • Interest Rate Risk: The debt portion of the portfolio, especially the Corporate Bond fund, is sensitive to changes in interest rates. If the central bank raises rates, the Net Asset Value (NAV) of existing bonds can fall, leading to temporary mark-to-market losses. Mitigation: This risk is managed by diversifying the debt holdings. The inclusion of the ICICI Prudential Short Term Fund, which has a lower duration, significantly dampens the portfolio's overall sensitivity to interest rate swings.
  • Inflation Risk: The primary threat to any long-term goal is inflation, which erodes the future purchasing power of the accumulated corpus. If actual inflation consistently exceeds the assumed 5.5%, the real value of the target amount could be diminished. Mitigation: The equity component, particularly the Mid Cap allocation, is specifically designed to generate returns that comfortably beat long-term inflation, thereby preserving and growing the real value of the investment.
  • Concentration Risk: Over-allocating to a single fund, sector, or asset class can expose the portfolio to idiosyncratic risks. Mitigation: This portfolio is well-diversified across five funds spanning four distinct asset categories (Large Cap, Mid Cap, Corporate Bond, Short Duration), significantly reducing concentration risk.
