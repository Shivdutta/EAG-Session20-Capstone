‚ö†Ô∏è 7. Risk Analysis

While the proposed portfolio is diversified and aligned with a moderate risk profile, it is imperative to understand the inherent risks. This strategy is subject to several factors that can impact performance. Market Risk is the most significant factor, as the 50% allocation to equity funds will be subject to market volatility. A broad market downturn could lead to a temporary decline in the portfolio's value. The inclusion of a Mid Cap fund (PGIM India Midcap Opportunities), while a growth driver, introduces higher volatility compared to the Large Cap anchor. Interest Rate Risk primarily affects the debt portion. A rise in interest rates can negatively impact the Net Asset Value (NAV) of debt funds, particularly the HDFC Corporate Bond Fund, as existing bonds with lower yields become less attractive. Credit Risk, though low, exists in the debt funds. This is the risk of an underlying corporate entity defaulting on its debt obligations, which would impact the fund's NAV. The selected funds mitigate this by investing in high-credit-quality (AA+ and above) paper. Finally, Fund Underperformance Risk is the possibility that a chosen fund may fail to perform in line with its benchmark or peers due to changes in management, strategy, or market conditions. Continuous monitoring and annual reviews are essential to mitigate these risks effectively.

Understanding the tax implications is essential for maximizing net returns.

  • Equity Funds Taxation: Investments held for over one year qualify for Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax. The first ‚Çπ1 lakh of gains in a financial year is tax-free. Gains above this amount are taxed at a flat rate of 10%.

  • ELSS Consideration: While not included in this core portfolio, the investor can consider redirecting up to ‚Çπ1.5 lakh of the annual equity investment into a top-tier Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) to avail tax benefits under Section 80C, if this deduction is not already fully utilized.
