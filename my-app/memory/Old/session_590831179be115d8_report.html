‚ö†Ô∏è 7. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

All investments are subject to risk, and it is crucial to understand the factors that could impact this plan. The strategy is designed to mitigate, but not eliminate, these risks through diversification, disciplined rebalancing, and a dynamic de-risking glide path. The primary risks are categorized below:

Market Risk: The 40% allocation to equity subjects the portfolio to market fluctuations. A prolonged bear market, especially in the initial years, could temporarily impact corpus growth. Mitigation: The 9-year horizon allows sufficient time for recovery from market downturns. The large 60% debt allocation provides a substantial cushion. Sticking to the monthly SIP discipline (Rupee Cost Averaging) is the most effective way to manage this risk.

Concentration & Volatility Risk: A significant 25% of the total portfolio is allocated to a single mid-cap fund. Mid-cap stocks are inherently more volatile than large-cap stocks and can experience sharper drawdowns. Mitigation: This is a calculated risk to target higher returns. The volatility is balanced by the stable large-cap fund and the dominant debt portion. Annual rebalancing will ensure this allocation does not grow disproportionately large.

Interest Rate & Credit Risk: The debt funds are subject to changes in interest rates (which can affect NAVs) and credit risk (the risk of a bond issuer defaulting). Mitigation: The portfolio uses a blend of Corporate Bond and Short Duration funds. The Short Duration fund has very low interest rate sensitivity. Both selected funds invest primarily in high-quality (AAA/AA+ rated) bonds, minimizing credit risk.
