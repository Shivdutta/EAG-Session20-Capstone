📝 Allocation Notes & Risk Profiles

The 'Moderate' allocation was chosen from a spectrum of risk-based strategies. The following guidelines illustrate the different approaches:

  • Very Low: Extremely conservative, e.g., 90% debt / 10% equity.
  • Low: Conservative, e.g., 70% debt / 30% equity.
  • Low-Moderate: Tilted conservative, e.g., 60% debt / 40% equity.
  • Moderate: Balanced mix, e.g., 50% equity / 50% debt.
  • High-Moderate: Tilted aggressive, e.g., 60% equity / 40% debt.
  • High: Aggressive, e.g., 70% equity / 30% debt.

⚖️ Risk Factors and Uncertainties

While this plan is built on sound financial principles, it is essential to acknowledge the inherent risks and uncertainties. The projected 10% annual return is not guaranteed and is subject to market performance. Equity markets can be volatile, and a significant downturn, especially closer to the 10-year horizon, could impact the final corpus. The assumed inflation rate of 5.5% is also a projection; should actual inflation run higher, the real purchasing power of the final amount could be diminished, potentially requiring an increase in SIP contributions. Furthermore, the debt portion of the portfolio, while safer than equity, is subject to interest rate risk; a rising interest rate environment can negatively affect the Net Asset Value (NAV) of bond funds. Fund manager performance and changes in investment mandates are additional variables that require periodic review. This plan should be considered a roadmap, not a certainty, and must be reviewed annually to adjust for changes in market conditions, personal financial situation, and progress towards the goal.
