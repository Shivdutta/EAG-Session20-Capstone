‚ö†Ô∏è Risk Analysis & Mitigation

All investments are subject to market risks, and this portfolio is no exception. A thorough understanding of these risks is essential for disciplined investing. The primary risks associated with this strategy are Market Risk, Interest Rate Risk, and Execution Risk.

Market Risk: The 50% allocation to equity, particularly the mid-cap fund, exposes the portfolio to market volatility. Mid-cap stocks can experience sharper drawdowns than large-cap stocks during market downturns. This risk is mitigated through the long 9-year horizon and the Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) approach, which averages the purchase cost over time. Furthermore, the inclusion of the Parag Parikh Flexi Cap fund, known for its better downside protection, helps cushion the portfolio.
Interest Rate Risk: The corporate bond funds are subject to interest rate risk. If the central bank raises interest rates, the Net Asset Value (NAV) of existing bonds may fall. However, this risk is mitigated as these funds hold bonds to maturity, and rising rates also mean new investments are made at higher yields. The high credit quality of the underlying bonds minimizes credit default risk.
Execution Risk: The success of this strategy, especially capital preservation, hinges on the disciplined execution of the de-risking glide path in years 7, 8, and 9. A failure to systematically shift from equity to debt could expose the accumulated corpus to a market downturn just before the goal is due. This risk is mitigated by setting calendar-based reminders and adhering strictly to the rebalancing and glide path schedule outlined in this report.
