‚öñÔ∏è Risk Analysis

Investing in mutual funds involves inherent risks that must be understood and managed. This strategy is designed to mitigate these risks, but they cannot be eliminated entirely. The primary risks associated with this portfolio include: Market Risk: The equity portion (50%) of the portfolio is exposed to market fluctuations. The glide path strategy is the primary tool to mitigate this risk by reducing equity exposure over time. Interest Rate Risk: Debt funds are sensitive to changes in interest rates. Inflation Risk: There is a risk that the investment returns may not keep pace with the rate of education inflation, which often exceeds general consumer inflation. The 50% allocation to growth-oriented equity funds is designed to counter this risk by aiming for higher long-term returns. Fund Manager Risk: The performance of an actively managed fund is heavily dependent on the fund manager's skill. A change in fund management or a period of underperformance relative to the benchmark can impact returns. Diversifying across five different funds from various AMCs helps to mitigate this risk.
