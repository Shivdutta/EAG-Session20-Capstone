The HDFC Corporate Bond fund is moderately exposed to this. The inclusion of the ICICI Prudential Short Term Fund, which has very low sensitivity to rate changes, helps dampen the overall portfolio's vulnerability to interest rate fluctuations.

Fund Underperformance: There is a risk that a selected fund may underperform its benchmark or peers due to changes in management, strategy, or market conditions. A defined monitoring framework with an annual performance review against benchmarks is critical to identify and address any consistent underperformance, potentially leading to a fund replacement.

Inflation Risk: This is the risk that the investment returns will not keep pace with the rate of education inflation, which is often higher than general inflation. This could lead to a shortfall in the target corpus. The strategy addresses this by including a high-growth Mid Cap fund and recommending a biennial review of the target corpus to adjust the SIP if needed.

A disciplined rebalancing strategy is crucial for managing risk and adhering to the investment plan over the long term.

  • Frequency & Schedule: The portfolio must be reviewed at the end of every investment year. It will be rebalanced back to its target asset allocation for that specific phase (e.g., 50/50 for years 1-6, 40/60 for year 7, etc.).
  • Trigger-Based Rebalancing: In addition to the annual review, a rebalance should be executed if market movements cause the allocation to either equity or debt to deviate by more than +/- 5% from its target (e.g., if equity grows to represent 55% of the portfolio value during the growth phase).
  • Execution Method: To minimize costs and tax events, rebalancing should be done in the following order of preference:
    1. Redirect SIPs: Temporarily adjust the monthly SIP allocation to invest more in the underweight asset class.
    2. Redemption & Re-investment: Only if SIP flows are insufficient to correct the imbalance, sell units from the overweight asset class and reinvest the proceeds into the underweight one.

The 9-year horizon is highly advantageous from a tax perspective, allowing for efficient management of capital gains.

  • Equity Investments (LTCG): Any gains from selling equity fund units held for more than 12 months are classified as Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG).   • Debt Investments (Indexation): Gains from debt funds held for more than 36 months are considered long-term. The 9-year horizon ensures the majority of debt holdings will qualify for this benefit.
  • Withdrawal Strategy: When redeeming funds, especially during the de-risking phase or for the final goal, plan withdrawals to spread capital gains across financial years if possible to maximize the use of the ‚Çπ1 lakh LTCG exemption annually.
