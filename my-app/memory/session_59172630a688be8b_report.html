üìö Glossary of Key Terms

  • CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate): The mean annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period longer than one year.
  • Standard Deviation (Std. Dev.): A statistical measure of market volatility, indicating how widely an investment's returns vary from its average return. A higher number implies higher risk.
  • Sharpe Ratio: A measure of risk-adjusted return. It describes how much excess return you receive for the extra volatility you endure for holding a riskier asset. A higher Sharpe Ratio is better.
  • Expense Ratio: The annual fee that all funds charge their shareholders. It is expressed as a percentage of assets.
  • SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): A method of investing a fixed sum, on a regular basis, in a mutual fund scheme.
