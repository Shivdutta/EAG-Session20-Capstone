Risk Profile Guidelines

The 'Moderate' selection implies a balanced 50/50 Equity/Debt split. This table shows how allocation shifts with different risk appetites:

  • Very Low: Extremely conservative, e.g., 90% debt / 10% equity
  • Low: Conservative, e.g., 70% debt / 30% equity
  • Low-Moderate: Tilted conservative, e.g., 60% debt / 40% equity
  • Moderate: Balanced mix, e.g., 50% equity / 50% debt
  • High-Moderate: Tilted aggressive, e.g., 60% equity / 40% debt
  • High: Aggressive, e.g., 70% equity / 30% debt
  • Very High: Extremely aggressive, e.g., 90% equity / 10% debt

üìâ Risk Factors and Uncertainties

Investing in capital markets involves inherent risks. While this plan is designed to align with a moderate risk profile, it is crucial to understand the following factors. The projected return of 10% is an estimate, not a guarantee; actual returns may vary significantly. Market Risk is the most prominent factor, as equity values can fluctuate due to economic conditions, political events, and investor sentiment, potentially leading to periods of negative returns. Execution Risk is currently the most critical and immediate concern for this specific plan; failing to select suitable debt funds for the 50% unallocated portion would invalidate the entire strategy, creating a high-risk, 100% equity portfolio. Furthermore, Inflation Risk poses a threat if the actual inflation rate exceeds the 5.5% assumption, which would erode the real value of the final corpus. For the debt portion, Interest Rate Risk can affect bond prices and fund NAVs. Finally, Fund Manager Risk exists for actively managed schemes, where performance is dependent on the expertise of the fund management team. Investors should regularly review their portfolio and rebalance if necessary to stay aligned with their financial goals and risk tolerance.
