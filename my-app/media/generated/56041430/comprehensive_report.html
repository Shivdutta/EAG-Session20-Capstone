Risk Factors and Uncertainties

Investing in financial markets involves risks, including market volatility, economic downturns, and unforeseen events. The actual returns may vary from the expected returns. Additionally, reliance on a limited fund shortlist from a single provider (PGIM India) can lead to sub-optimal diversification and increased concentration risk. The absence of indicative ratings for the funds further compounds the risk, as it necessitates a more rigorous due diligence process. Inflation rates could deviate significantly from the assumed 5.5%, impacting the real value of the accumulated assets. Changes in tax laws could also affect the returns from investments. Furthermore, the models used for SIP calculation and inflation adjustment rely on historical data and assumptions, which might not accurately predict future outcomes. The investor should be aware that financial models are not guarantees of future success and may be subject to errors or limitations. A comprehensive risk assessment, either automated or manual, should be regularly performed to adapt to changing market conditions and personal circumstances. The information provided should not be considered as financial advice, and consulting a qualified financial advisor is essential.
