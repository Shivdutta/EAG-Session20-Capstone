üìâ Risk Factors and Uncertainties

All investments carry inherent risks. This plan is subject to several factors that could impact the final outcome. It is crucial to understand these uncertainties before proceeding.

  • Execution Risk: The most significant and immediate risk is the identified failure of the upstream data pipeline. Without reliable data for fund selection, the entire strategy is undermined. Choosing funds without quantitative analysis turns a strategic plan into guesswork and exposes the investment to significant underperformance. This issue must be rectified before any capital is deployed.
  • Market Risk: The 10% annualized return is a projection, not a guarantee. Equity markets are inherently volatile and can experience prolonged downturns. The value of the portfolio will fluctuate, and there may be periods where it is significantly below the projected path. The 9-year horizon helps mitigate this risk, but it cannot eliminate it.
  • Fund Selection Risk: Even within a well-defined category, the performance gap between the best and worst funds can be substantial. Poorly chosen funds with high expense ratios, inconsistent performance, or inexperienced management will lead to underperformance against the benchmark and jeopardize the financial goal. This highlights the criticality of the failed data retrieval step.
  • Inflation Risk: The plan assumes a 5.5% average annual inflation rate. If actual inflation runs consistently higher, the real value of the target corpus will be eroded, and the final amount may be insufficient to meet the intended goal. The plan may need to be revisited to increase the SIP amount if inflation proves to be persistently high.
