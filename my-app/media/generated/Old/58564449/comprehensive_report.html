üìâ Risk Factors and Uncertainties

This investment plan, while structured for a 'moderate' risk profile, is subject to various market risks and uncertainties. It is essential to understand these factors. The 10% projected annual return is an assumption, not a guarantee; actual returns may be higher or lower. The equity portion (50%) is exposed to market risk, meaning its value will fluctuate with stock market movements, and could experience periods of negative returns. While a 10-year horizon helps mitigate short-term volatility, a significant market downturn near the retirement date could impact the final corpus. The debt portion (50%) is subject to interest rate risk and credit risk. If interest rates rise, the value of existing bonds may fall. Credit risk is the possibility that a corporate bond issuer could default on its payments. Furthermore, inflation risk remains a constant; if inflation averages higher than the projected 5.5%, the real value of the final corpus will be eroded. This plan's success depends on the discipline to remain invested through market cycles.
