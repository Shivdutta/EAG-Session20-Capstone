Risk Factors and Uncertainties

Investing in mutual funds carries inherent risks. Equity funds, particularly small-cap and mid-cap funds, are subject to market volatility and may experience significant fluctuations in value. Debt funds are exposed to credit risk and interest rate risk, which can negatively impact returns. The 'Very High' risk factors mentioned in the notes for equity funds indicate the potential for substantial losses. Investment decisions should be based on a thorough understanding of the risks involved and consultation with a qualified financial advisor. Past performance is not indicative of future results, and returns may vary significantly. The fund data from T012, with its completeness and reliability limitations, underscores the need for independent verification from reputable sources before making investment decisions. External events such as changes in economic conditions, regulatory policies, or company-specific developments could also affect fund performance. As such, diversification is paramount to manage overall portfolio risk, and ongoing monitoring is essential to promptly address any deviations from the intended investment strategy.

Specific Risk Factors:

  • Market Volatility: Equity markets can be unpredictable and subject to sharp declines.
  • Credit Risk: Debt funds are exposed to the risk that the issuer may default on its obligations.
  • Interest Rate Risk: Changes in interest rates can negatively impact the value of debt funds.
  • Inflation Risk: Unexpected increases in inflation can erode the real value of investments.
  • Liquidity Risk: Some investments may be difficult to sell quickly at a fair price.

Uncertainties:

  • Future Inflation Rates: The actual inflation rate may differ from the assumed rate of 5.5%, affecting the real value of returns.
  • Market Returns: Actual market returns may vary from the assumed monthly return rate of 1%.
  • Regulatory Changes: Changes in government policies and regulations can impact investment returns.
  • Economic Conditions: Economic downturns and recessions can negatively affect investment performance.
