Investing in financial markets involves inherent risks. The projected SIP returns are based on certain assumptions, and actual returns may vary. Key risk factors include:

  • Market Risk: Fluctuations in the stock market can impact the value of equity investments.
  • Interest Rate Risk: Changes in interest rates can affect the returns on debt investments.
  • Inflation Risk: Higher-than-expected inflation can erode the real value of investments.
  • Liquidity Risk: Certain investments may be difficult to sell quickly without incurring a loss.
  • Credit Risk: The risk that a borrower may default on debt obligations.
  • Reinvestment Risk: The risk that future returns may be lower when reinvesting proceeds from maturing investments.

It is important to regularly review and rebalance the portfolio to mitigate these risks. Market conditions and fund performance can change over time. Diversification within each asset class is important. Investment decisions should be made after consulting with a qualified financial advisor.
